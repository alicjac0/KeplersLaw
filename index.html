Kepler's Law
  • Law of Ellipses: All planets move in elliptical orbits, with the Sun located at one of the two foci. This means planetary orbits are not perfect circles but "flattened" circles.
  • Law of Equal Areas: An imaginary line connecting a planet to the Sun sweeps out equal areas in equal intervals of time. Consequently, a planet moves faster when it is closer to the Sun (perihelion) and slower when it is farther away (aphelion).
  • Law of Harmonies: The square of the orbital period of a planet is directly proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis of its orbit, expressed as T2 ∝ a3. This means planets farther from the Sun take significantly longer to complete an orbit than closer planets.
